Academic Life

I study Control and Automation Engineering at the University of Brasília. The course itself has a lot of content. That is because the way this course is handled in my country is basically a mix of Electrical and Mechanical Engineering plus Computer Science. So yeah, we have to cover a lot. Due to this, the course has a flexible work load per semester. The only requirement is that we must finish in a period from 5 to 10 years. Currently, I'm in my 6th year.
There are different reasons why people take longer to graduate. In my case, during my sixth semester I took two simultaneous internships, which totaled at 32 work hours per week. So I took a smaller academic work load for one year to compensate. But that doesn't mean that in this period I stalled academically. Here is a couple of things I have accomplished in this scenario:

Tutoring
Universities are spaces that provide a lot of interaction between people that are in different steps in their courses. It isn't uncommon for universities in Brazil to create resources to provide more interaction between newcomers and their seniors. Some of the more difficult courses have special tutoring programs, in which people who already passed the course are hired to help new students with their projects and questions they might have. I worked as a tutor in the course "Algorithms and Computer Programming". This is the first programming course in all computer-related courses.
Research project
My first research project was related to Industrial Robotics. In the mechanics lab there sat a very lonely robot from the 90s called ABB IRB 2000, because its main control board got fried. So the proposal was to retrofit the robot using a more modern direct-to-PC control architecture that allowed for GCode input, with LinuxCNC.
Brasília's Open Laboratory (LAB)
This is a multi-course laboratory in which the purpose is to train students to work in the industry as Engineers. The focus is on helping students learn to work on a multidisciplinary setting on engineering projects. These projects are developed by request of either enterprises whose objective is to solve real world problems, or the academic community itself. In order to provide solutions, we work with fast prototyping technologies, such as 3D printing, lasercutting and 3D scanning. Thus, in this space we function much like a real company, with an operations team and a management team, client meetings, maintenance schedule (which is performed by the students themselves) and project management timelines. As part of the operations team, my tasks ranged from designing the solution itself through CAE and CAD software, as well as work and troubleshoot the machines. Over the months, I got a lot of experience with maintenance of 3D printing systems in many configurations. There are some cool things in the Gallery, so feel free to check them out.
